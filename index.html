Why we built this

I'm leaving for graduate school
My wife needed her village.

"In July 2025, I leave for graduate school. My wife Danielle will be home in Waller, Texas — with two young kids and a new baby due in June 2026. The people who love her want to help. But nobody had a way to actually show up in a coordinated, meaningful way. So I built one."

M
Marques Moore
Founder · YourKitchen 2026

YourKitchen started as a gift for Danielle. A way for her village — family, friends, colleagues — to claim dates on a calendar and send dinner without her having to ask. No coordination overhead. No group texts. No three lasagnas on the same Tuesday. Just dinner, handled, by the people who love her.

Then we realized: every family going through something hard needs exactly this.

How it works

Three steps.
One less thing to worry about.

1

Someone creates a Kitchen

A friend, family member, or the recipient sets up a YourKitchen calendar — choosing how many months of support they need, their meal preferences, allergies, and preferred delivery window.

5 minutes to set up
2

The village claims dates

Friends and family get a link to the calendar. They pick a date, choose a meal from a curated local restaurant list, leave a personal note — and save their card. No awkward payment requests. No forgotten commitments.

One tap to claim a date
3

The recipient confirms and eats

On the day, the recipient gets a text: "Marcus picked dinner for tonight — does this work?" They reply Y to confirm or swap to something else. The order is placed automatically. Dinner arrives.

Y to confirm · N to swap
